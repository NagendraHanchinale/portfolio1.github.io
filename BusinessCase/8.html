Ride-pooling aims to match multiple riders traveling in similar directions into the same vehicle, reducing total fleet size, travel time, and carbon footprint. Efficient optimization ensures minimal inconvenience to users while maximizing vehicle utilization.

üß© Core Problem

  • Minimize additional travel (detour) introduced when pooling riders
  • Respect vehicle capacity constraints (e.g., max 4 riders)
  • Ensure rider wait times remain below 5 minutes
  • Handle >100,000 concurrent ride requests in real time

The objective is to reduce the total system detour, measured by the difference between pooled route times and each rider's solo trip time.

This method checks if a new rider can be inserted into an existing vehicle route without exceeding detour and time constraints. If the insertion is feasible, the route is updated; otherwise, a new vehicle is dispatched.

2Ô∏è‚É£ Genetic Algorithm for Batch Optimization

Genetic algorithms work on batches of requests. They evolve combinations of rider-to-vehicle assignments over generations using selection, crossover, and mutation. The fittest solutions (i.e., lowest detour) survive and improve iteratively.

By combining fast heuristics for real-time matching with global optimization techniques like genetic algorithms, ride-pooling systems can balance user satisfaction and operational efficiency at scale. This reduces idle vehicles, lowers emissions, and makes urban transport more sustainable.
